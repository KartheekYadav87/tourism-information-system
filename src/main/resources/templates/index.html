Welcome to Tamil Nadu

The Land of Temples, Culture & Natural Beauty

Tamil Nadu, the southernmost state of peninsular India, is a vibrant tapestry of ancient civilization, spiritual devotion, and breathtaking natural landscapes. With a coastline stretching over 1,076 km, towering Dravidian temple gopurams soaring into the sky, and dense wildlife reserves in the Nilgiris, the state offers every kind of travel experience imaginable.

Home to 39 UNESCO recognized monuments, the cradle of Carnatic music and Bharatanatyam dance, and one of the world's oldest living languages — Tamil — this remarkable state invites you to discover a civilization that has flourished for over 2,000 years.

Essential Tips

Travel Smart in Tamil Nadu

A little preparation goes a long way when exploring Tamil Nadu. Here are our top tips to make your journey smooth, safe, and unforgettable.

Read All Guides
Best Time to Visit

October to March is ideal for most destinations. Hill stations are pleasant year-round. Waterfalls are spectacular during monsoons (July–Sept).

Food & Cuisine

Try traditional Tamil meals on banana leaves, Chettinad cuisine, filter coffee, and fresh seafood. Stick to busy stalls for safe street food.

What to Wear

Dress modestly at temples — cover shoulders and knees. Carry a light jacket for hill stations. Comfortable sandals are essential for temple visits.

Emergency Contacts

Tourist Helpline: 1800-425-4747 (Free, 24x7). Police: 100. Ambulance: 108. Women Helpline: 181. Save these before you travel.
